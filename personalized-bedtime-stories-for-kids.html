What they are

Bedtime stories that begin with your child.

A personalized bedtime story is built around real details from your family instead of a one-size-fits-all plot. The child can become the explorer, the helper, the inventor, or the brave friend at the center of the adventure.

Personal details

Familiar names, interests, and routines

Children often lean in when a story includes something they recognize: a favorite animal, a hobby, a mood they understand, or a small bedtime ritual that feels like home.

Parent-guided

A story shaped by grown-up choices

MoonTale keeps parents in the loop by letting them choose the age, theme, tone, reading length, and learning goal before the story is created.

How MoonTale works

Simple inputs become a story made for tonight.

Language woven in gently

New words inside a story they already care about.

Language learning

A soft way to introduce new languages at bedtime.

MoonTale can include a small set of new words in languages such as English, Polish, Spanish, French, or German. The main story follows the current language you choose, while the learning-language setting can add vocabulary as a gentle discovery layer.

This keeps language exposure warm and low-pressure. Children hear new words in context, and parents get a simple prompt for one tiny bedtime sentence after reading.

For parents

Useful when bedtime needs calm, not another search.

Parents can quickly create a story that fits the child's age, energy, interests, and learning goal. MoonTale is designed for shared reading: a parent-guided moment that can make bedtime feel more connected, more imaginative, and easier to repeat.
